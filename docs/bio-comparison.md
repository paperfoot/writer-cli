# Boris Djordjevic — Bio Comparison

## Original

Boris Djordjevic is the Founder and CEO of 199 Biotechnologies, a vertically integrated longevity company developing epigenetic reprogramming therapies — engineered Yamanaka factors being validated in glioblastoma before targeting aging itself. Through 199 Labs, the company manufactures GMP-grade Procyanidin C1 and is developing novel classes of universal natural senolytics.

Trained in medicine, Boris has spent fifteen years working to get frontier aging science out of the lab and into people's lives. The 199 ecosystem now spans 199 Clinic (a longevity medical concierge that takes on patients who have exhausted conventional options), 199 Diagnostics (biological age quantification), Healtrix (clinical assessment platform), and Triple Helix (gene therapy delivery and expression).

His research, conducted in collaboration with University College London, has been published in Nature Methods, Science Advances, Advanced Science, and other leading journals — spanning mechanobiology, cancer metastasis, neurodegeneration, and longevity science. He is the author of The Hands That Wouldn't Change and The Bicycle Ride, and an Oxford All-Innovate award winner.

Beyond the core biotech work, Boris builds AI infrastructure for biological research — purpose-built machine learning for genomics and proteomics through PaperFoot AI, and an extension of the longevity mission to companion animals with Petus AI. He maintains 56 open-source repositories across the 199-biotechnologies and 199-mcp GitHub organisations, building agentic AI tools and research infrastructure in Rust.

Boris speaks English, Mandarin, and French. He is based between London and Singapore.
